Matter Assemblers are machines that can fabricate objects by assembling atoms into complex structures. The assembler searches through a massive database to find out exactly which atoms it needs for the requested item. For example, if you wanted glass, the assembler determines that it needs silicon, oxygen, and trace amounts of sodium, calcium, boron. It then draws said atoms from a Source Network, which is basically a supply chain that delivers neccessary elements to where they are needed, and rapidly positions and bonds these atoms in the correct sequence to form the desired product.
