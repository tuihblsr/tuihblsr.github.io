Living Metal, scientifically referred to as Vivos Metallum, is a remarkable virus-like mineral that possesses the ability to perform basic movements and undergo self-repair.

When the metal is heated above 5000 K, its ability to regenerate begins to worsen, and by 7000 K, its self-repair functions cease entirely. When exposed to temperatures around 3000 K, the "genetic" structure of the metal starts to degrade and "mutations" occur. These mutations can cause alterations in its properties or structure, whether good or bad.
