Main Chronological Order Rando

1. Jules joins The Silver Claws

• 1050 words

When she was little, Jules promised herself that she would never, ever join a faction. After all, it was a faction that had killed her parents and ruined her life. However, past self did not account for the fact that she might get hunted down after failing to pay back a loan. After taking a bullet to the leg, she reluctantly concluded that joining a faction was the only way to not die horribly. She spotted a pedestrian passing by, their eyes glued to the glowing screen of a Newstron - one of those digital newspapers that was basically 90% ads and 10% random clickbait about UFOs and celebrities. Her hand shot out and snatched the Newstron right from their grasp. "Hey!" the pedestrian yelled, but Jules was already limping away. She swiped through the ads until one particular headline caught her eye, “The Steel Claws is opening up applications! Join one of the top factions in the world now!”

"Guess it’s not like I have much of a choice," she mumbled under her breath and pressed the “Apply Now” button. The application process was surprisingly simple: name, age, profession. She hesitated only briefly before typing in the bare essentials. Her finger hovered over the final section. “Reason for applying.” She frowned at the screen, wondering what could possibly sound convincing enough. In the end, she simply wrote, “I need protection. I’ll do whatever.”

A couple minutes later a drone flew over and dropped a tiny package at her feet. Inside was an envelope and a syringe containing a blue liquid.
> Meet at Sector 22, Dock 3. Midnight. Bring your belongings. Be ready.
> P.S. The syringe will heal your bullet woundVortexhydrobenzoextraflexolumine is a blue liquid that when injected into a biological creature, rapidly accelerates its healing rate. It was invented by The Silver Claws.
Jules stared at the card for a long moment, thinking. How did they know about the bullet wound? Jules glanced at the syringe, its contents glowing faintly under the dim streetlights. She shrugged and plunged the needle into her leg. Almost immediately, the pain in her leg dulled and the wound began to knit itself together. She let out a whistle, amazed. Maybe being in a faction isn’t the worst idea. I wonder if they’ll even give me some implants. Wouldn’t that be nice?

Sector 22 wasn’t too far away, and Jules didn’t have many possessions, just her energy rifle, teddy bear (hey it had sentimental value), and some supplies. As she neared the sector, the towering skyline of the city began to fade into an industrial wasteland. She set up a temporary camp just outside the dock, and waited.

Midnight came, and the quiet was broken by the soft hum of a hovercraftHovercraft are flying helicopter/car vehicles used for quick transportation in dangerous or high-risk areas. approaching from the distance. It landed softly on the cracked pavement, the doors opening with a soft hiss. A figure walked out, clad in shining power armour. They motioned towards the craft, “Get in. If you survive the initiation, you’ll have a place among the elite. Don't blow your only chance.” Jules hesitated, and then stepped in. The hum of its engine vibrated through the floor, and Jules instinctively reached out to steady herself as the craft lifted off the ground. “Where are we headed?” she asked, trying to break the silence. There was a pause and then, “To the faction HQ, now shut your mouth.”

After what felt like eternity, the craft finally landed in front of a small cabin. The figure motioned for Jules to follow them in, and pressed a button. There was a small zip! and suddenly she was in a massive reception room. The pilot guy was gone and so she tentatively walked up to the front desk. A tall, broad-shouldered man with a scar running down his face, looked up as they entered. "Ah, the new recruit," he said, his voice deep and gravelly. “Let’s see if you can pass the initiation.” He stood up, his chair scraping against the floor with an unpleasant screech, “Follow me.” Jules did as instructed, following him through a maze of halls that looked far too clean and well-maintained for a faction that was supposedly made up of mercenaries, assassins, and soldiers. The walls were lined with military gear, weapons, and syringes. They stopped at a heavy door that slid open with a pneumatic hiss. Inside was a circular room, dimly lit, with a single chair in the center. The man gestured for her to sit. The door slid shut behind her, and she heard the faint click of a lock engaging.

“First stage," the man’s voice came through a speaker, “Survive.” A soft whirring sound filled the room, and suddenly a screen flickered to life in front of her. A digital display appeared, showing a countdown timer. Shadowy figures emerged from the corners, cloaked in dark gear, their faces hidden beneath helmets. The first figure lunged at her with a blade. Jules didn’t hesitate. She rolled out of the way, her energy rifle springing into her hands with practiced ease, and fired a shot. The figure went down with a thud. She fired shot after shot, until she felt a searing pain in her back. Jules staggered and whirled around, firing wildly. The figure tackled her to the ground, knocking the rifle out of her hands. She shrieked and ripped the knife out of their hands, stabbing instinctively. The figure went slacked, and she snatched up her rifle, spotting a small control panel on the wall. She fumbled at the buttons, fully aware that her time was running out.

There was a click and then the figures vanished. Jules collapsed to her knees, gasping for breath. The pain in her back throbbed, but the adrenaline still surged through her veins, keeping her on high alert. “Not bad,” the man’s voice came through the speaker once more, his tone somewhat approving, and another one of those blue syringes fell from the ceiling. “Stage two, get ready.” Jules snatched the syringe up and stabbed it into her arm without hesistation as she felt the walls shift. A digital map flickered to life in front of her, seeming to always appear 3 feet ahead of her. It displayed a grid of interconnected rooms with different color-coded zones, some flashing red, others green. A voice crackled through the speaker: “Stage two: Stealth challenge. Navigate the maze. Avoid detection. If you get caught, you fail.”
