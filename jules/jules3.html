Jules opened her eyes. Where am I? Silvery control panels lined the walls of the dimly lit space, and there was a medicinal smell to everything. She sat up. At least she tried to sit up, but her right arm offered no assistance. It simply lay at her side for a moment before moving, as if it was on a delay. She tried to wipe the sweat from her forehead, but once again, her arm took a moment to do it. “You are conscious ahead of schedule,” a mechanical voice said, but there was no one else in the room. "What's wrong with my arm?" Jules asked, groggily.

“That is not your arm,” the voice replied.

"What?" Jules willed her arm to move. A moment passed, and then it positioned itself in front of her. It was identical to her left arm except, when she held it up to the light, the arm was semitransparent. The light passed through her skin, making it appear grayer and slightly blue. She could see shiny silver bones, flickering lights, and computer hardware. "What the-!?"

"The arm is a prototype cybernetic enhancement designed for optimal function. Your original limb was beyond repair after the Guardian's assault. After we finish your implants, your arm will be better than your original in almost every way." Jules felt a wave of anger-and awe-at the arm. She had always hated factions, and now here she was, becoming part of one in the most intrusive way possible. "I didn’t ask for any of this," she said angrily.

"The choice is not yours to make. We cannot afford to have our members impaired in any way. Your neural implants will be activated now, suspending consciousness." And then everything went dark.
