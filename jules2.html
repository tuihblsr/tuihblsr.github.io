Jules groaned. She wasn't all that good at stealth and her energy rifle was loud. The digital map flickered again, highlighting a path through the maze of rooms. The green zones seemed to be safe, while the red ones... those were probably the ones to avoid. A soft beep sounded as the countdown resumed. Two minutes. Great. She moved carefully at first, sticking to the edges of the maze, her rifle slung across her back. She knew she couldn’t risk firing it; the noise would alert the guards, and she'd be finished before she could get anywhere. The first half of the maze was quite easy. But then she turned the corner, and there. Another red zone. There was no way to sneak past this one. The only option was to wait it out. She stayed perfectly still, holding her breath, watching the timer tick down with a feeling of dread.

And then she heard footsteps heading in the direction of her hiding spot. Jules panicked and looked around wildly, looking for an escape. There. A small access hatch built into the floor, just wide enough for her to fit through. It wasn’t much, but it was her best shot. The cover opened with a loud clang! and Jules dove in as she heard shots being fired, barely missing her. She crawled through the tunnel, her hands shaking. The countdown had already hit one minute. Jules cursed and glanced around the tunnel. It was narrow and cramped, but there was a faint glow ahead-maybe another access door or a way out. As she neared the glow however, she saw the telltale outlines of a pressure plate. Great, she thought, booby traps. They really don’t make this easy.

Jules inched backwards and, with some difficulty, unslung the rifle from her back and fired a shot. The plate sizzled, and then died. She breathed a sigh of relief and started crawling again, rifle back on her back. She was beginning to feel the effects of the earlier adrenaline rush wearing off, her limbs aching and her chest tight from the constant tension. Finally, she reached another hatch, this one slightly larger, and it didn’t look rigged with a pressure plate. She hesitated, listening carefully for any sounds above, but there was nothing. The countdown on her map showed only 30 seconds remaining. No time to waste. Jules pushed open the hatch and slid into the next room. It was smaller and looked like some kind of maintenance area, pipes and wires running along the ceiling and walls. There was a door at the far end. It was locked, but the map on her display showed her just a few meters from her goal. Only one more obstacle. And this one would be a doozy.

“Stage three: The Final Challenge. Defeat the Guardian. If you make it out, you're in. Fail... and it’s all over. Good luck, recruit.” There was a click! and the door swung open. In the center of the room stood a towering figure, holding a massive, glowing sword. She raised her rifle, taking aim at the Guardian’s head. She squeezed the trigger but the bolt seemed to have no real impact. The Guardian charged at her, moving with a speed that belied its massive size. Jules barely had time to dive out of the way as the sword swung down with a thunderous crack. The force of the blow sent a shockwave through the room, and she slammed into a wall.

Without a rifle that could pierce the armor, she was at a severe disadvantage. But she wasn’t completely defenseless. She could still try to outsmart this thing. She glanced around the room, taking in the surroundings. Pipes, machinery, exposed wires… If she could disable some of the power, maybe she could weaken the Guardian. The Guardian turned, its helmet swiveling as it tracked her movement, sword raised and ready for the next strike. She sprinted toward the wall, using her rifle to fire off a few bolts to distract the Guardian. She dove behind a pile of crates, watching as the Guardian took the bait and charged straight toward her. Jules yanked out a grenade from her utility belt and set it to a short timer, then tossed it toward the wires. As it rolled across the floor, she bolted for cover, ducking behind the crates once again.

The blast fried the exposed circuits, causing the room’s lights to flicker and the humming of the Guardian’s armor to falter for just a second. In that brief moment, Jules dashed forward, grabbing a heavy metal pipe from the ground and swinging it toward the Guardian’s knee joint. The pipe slammed into the weak spot, and she heard a satisfying crunch. The Guardian hissed and swung it's blade, catching Jules in her right arm, completely slicing it off. She howled with pain and rolled out of the way of another strike. She crawled towards her rifle, and fired. The shot caught it right between the eyes and it collasped. She heard, "Stage three, complete. Medical Attention required immediately. Welcome to The Steel Claws, newbie," right before losing consciousness.
