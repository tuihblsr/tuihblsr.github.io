Lo's main source of income was mugging. Most people, as soon as they saw his shotgun, immediately handed over their money and then ran off in terror. But one day, he decided to target a wealthy-looking man. Lo strolled over to him, flicking on his sights. He expected the usual response-a yelp of terror, the scrambling to give up cash, and maybe even a few desperate pleas. Instead, the man simply smiled, unfazed, and said, "What can I do for you, sir?" After a beat, Lo snapped out of his confusion, regaining his usual confidence. He motioned for the man to hand over his wallet, but the man didn't even flinch. With slow precision, he reached into his jacket pocket and pulled out a thick wallet. Without a second thought, he handed it over, his smile still in place.

Three days later, Lo was hanging around the bar, drinking, when he overhead someone say, "I've never seen so many Silver Claws here before! Apparently some guy tried to mug a member of the Silver Claws. He really fucked up." Lo froze mid-sip, the words Silver Claws sinking into his gut like a rock. He had heard of them, of course-their reputation was impossible to ignore. He downed the rest of his drink in one gulp and tossed a few credits on the table before getting up quickly. He needed to leave. Now. Lo's heart raced as he stumbled out of the bar, his mind wild. The wealthy man he'd targeted-he had't been just some rich guy; he was a member of the Silver Claws. He needed to get out of the sector, and fast.

His thoughts were interrupted by the faint sound of footsteps behind him. He cursed and ducked into a narrow passageway, hoping to lose whoever was following him. There was a beeping noise and then, "He's somewhere near. Find and capture him." Before he could make another move, a heavy hand landed on his shoulder, spinning him around. A figure in dark power armour, face obscured by a helmet, stood in front of him. Lo's heart skipped a beat. He had been caught. “Don't make this harder than it has to be,” the figure said in a calm, monotone voice. "Wait hold on I didn't know!" Lo stammered, trying to appeal to whatever mercy they might have. “I didn't know he was-”

"I don't want to hear your excuses," the figure interrupted, hauling him through the alley. "You stole from the wrong person. Now you'll pay the price." The next thing Lo knew, he was being shoved into a sleek, silver hovercraft. A voice crackled over an intercom. “Subject apprehended. Preparing for transport. Default Protocal 150”
